## Why These Errors Occurred

### 1. Pareidolia
The human tendency to see patterns in random data. Short 3-letter words like "CFB", "KEY", "YOU" have relatively high probability of appearing by chance in 7,000+ characters.

### 2. Confirmation Bias
Initial excitement about finding a creator's signature ("CFB") led to accepting weak evidence without statistical validation.

### 3. Multiple Testing Problem
Testing 20+ decoding methods on the same data increases false positive rate. Without Bonferroni correction, coincidental patterns appear significant.

### 4. Data Processing Errors
The original extraction method may have introduced artifacts that were misinterpreted as messages.

## Methodology Lesson

For future research:
1. **Always run Monte-Carlo validation** before claiming significance
2. **Report p-values** for all statistical claims
3. **Document negative results** as rigorously as positive ones
4. **Correct the record** when claims are disproven
5. **Apply Bonferroni correction** for multiple testing
