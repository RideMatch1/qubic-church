## Why XOR 127?

XOR 127 is mathematically significant:
- 127 = 2^7 - 1 (Mersenne prime)
- 127 is the maximum positive value in signed 8-bit integer
- XOR 127 effectively "flips" all 7 lower bits
- This encoding aligns with the matrix's 127-based architecture

The choice of XOR 127 as the encoding method is consistent with the overall mathematical design of the Anna Matrix.
