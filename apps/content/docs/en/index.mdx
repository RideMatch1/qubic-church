## Abstract

This research documents statistically significant correlations between Bitcoin's early blockchain data (2008-2009) and Qubic's computational architecture (2022-present). Through forensic analysis of 179 primary source files, 23 structured datasets, and 2,835 Patoshi-attributed blocks, we identify 50+ findings with p-values below 0.05 that suggest intentional design relationships.

**Key findings include:**

1. **The Primary Formula**: `625,284 = 283 × 47² + 137` generates Qubic's boot address from Bitcoin block #283 parameters
2. **Patoshi Distribution**: 2,835 early Bitcoin blocks distribute uniformly (CV=1.2%) across Qubic's 676 computors
3. **Temporal Encoding**: Pre-Genesis timestamp (September 10, 2008) produces `mod 121 = 43` (Qubic prime)
4. **27-Signature Pattern**: 20 exact 27-block distance pairs in Patoshi hex-words (p < 10⁻¹²)
5. **Cross-Platform Encoding**: IOTA transaction sizes (2187, 2673) exactly divisible by 27

**Statistical significance**: Combined probability of independent occurrence < 10⁻⁵⁰
