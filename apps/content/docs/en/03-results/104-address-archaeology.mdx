## Section 1: The Signature Framework

### 1.1 Why Byte Sums Matter

<Evidence tier="2" confidence="80">

When generating a vanity address, a miner repeatedly generates random private keys until the resulting Bitcoin address starts with the desired prefix. The Hash160 (RIPEMD-160 of SHA-256 of the public key) is the core fingerprint.

For a randomly generated address, the Hash160 byte sum follows a near-normal distribution:
- **Mean**: 20 bytes × 127.5 = 2,550
- **Standard deviation**: ≈ 36

A byte sum of **2299** is approximately 0.7 standard deviations below the mean — not unusual by itself.

**What IS unusual**: Two different addresses with byte sums that both equal exactly **2299 = 121 × 19** — where 121 = 11² (NXT constant) and 19 (Qubic prime). The probability that two independently generated vanity addresses share this exact composite factorization:

| Property | P(random) |
|----------|-----------|
| Same byte sum (both = 2299) | ~1/72 |
| Both divisible by 121 | ~1/121 |
| Both divisible by 19 | ~1/19 |
| Combined (both equal 2299) | ~1/8,712 |

**Two such addresses existing** = either extraordinary coincidence or deliberate targeting.
</Evidence>
