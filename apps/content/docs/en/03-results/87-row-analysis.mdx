## Key Findings

<Callout type="warning" title="Previous Documentation Was Misleading">
Previous documentation focused on Row 6 as "the Oracle Row" with 24/128 value-26 cells (18.8%). However, three other rows are MORE biased:

| Row | Value-26 Count | Percentage | Entropy |
|-----|---------------|------------|---------|
| **23** | **29/128** | **22.7%** | 4.59 |

Row 6 IS statistically significant (p < 0.0001 vs random rows), but it is **not unique** in its bias. **8 rows total** have value 26 as their most common value.
</Callout>

### 2. Value-26 Bias is Systematic

The rows with value 26 as their dominant value are: **6, 7, 23, 41, 53, 55, 61, 63**.

This suggests the value-26 concentration is a **structural property of the matrix**, not a special feature of any single row. The previous narrative about Row 6 being "the Oracle Row" overemphasized one row while missing the broader pattern.
